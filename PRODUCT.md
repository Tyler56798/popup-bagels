# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The PopUp Bagels franchise growth team (currently Tyler, potentially 2-3 teammates later) doing office-building outreach in downtown Chicago. Context: desk research and route planning on a laptop, plus on-foot reference during walking days. They live in tools like monday.com and expect that vocabulary: boards, colorful status labels, dense tables, fast inline editing.

## Product Purpose

A focused CRM for booking pop-up bagel events in office buildings: track 88 target buildings through an outreach pipeline (not contacted → reached out → followed up → booked/declined), manage contacts and follow-ups, plan walking routes on a map, and review conversion analytics. Success = more booked events with less dropped follow-up.

## Brand Personality

Energetic, organized, work-OS. The interface should feel like monday.com: bright white surfaces, one confident purple-blue accent, and saturated solid status colors that make pipeline state readable at a glance. Friendly but businesslike; the fun lives in the color system, not in decoration.

## Anti-references

- The previous design of this app: muted stone/amber earth tones, dark ink sidebar, emoji icons. All of it is being replaced.
- Emojis anywhere in the UI. Icons are drawn (SVG), never typed.
- Generic gray "enterprise admin" dashboards with washed-out pastel badges.
- Dark-mode-by-default developer-tool aesthetics (Linear-style). This is a bright, daylight work OS.

## Design Principles

1. **Status is color, color is status.** Saturated solid label colors carry pipeline meaning everywhere (table, board, map, calendar) and mean the same thing on every screen. Color is never decoration.
2. **White is the workspace.** Content sits on white; chrome (sidebar, toolbars) sits on a barely-gray second neutral. No dark panels.
3. **Boards over pages.** Tables and kanban are the primary surfaces; they should feel dense, alive, and inline-editable, like a monday board, not a report.
4. **Familiar beats clever.** Standard work-OS affordances (left nav, top bar, pills, avatars). The tool disappears into the outreach task.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. White text on status colors must clear contrast at label sizes (use monday's darker tones where needed). Visible focus rings on all interactive elements. Respect prefers-reduced-motion. Color is always paired with a text label (pills carry words, not just hue).
